Our history

Who we are?

Kittysaver is the global engine of change for cats. For more than 30 years, we've inspired individuals and communities to work to change laws, improve shelters, and most importantly save cats.

More than half thousand supporters help us in the movement to protect cats' lives. We advance progressive policies for cats in communities all over Belgium and across the globe and work toward a world that values the lives of all animals.

From our headquarters our staff serve as the voice for cats and the millions of people who value them.

Take a Walk Through Our History

In this age of viral cat videos and kitten memes for every occasion, it's no surprise that cats have a saint of their own. The patron saint of cats is a woman named Gertrude of Nivelles. She appears as a youthful, haloed nun cradling a cat in her arms like a baby.

There's no official documentation or old tradition naming St. Gertrude of Nivelles as the patron saint of cats. Considering the Church's checkered history of its treatment of cats, cats have long deserved and needed a patron saint of their own.

Cats were ideal and valued companions for monks and nuns in the Middle Ages. St. Gertrude of Nivelles is a good fitting human counterpart to the domestic cat, a creature known for being resolutely independent, and prone to mysterious habits that never make sense to its human owner, who loves and accepts cats as they are anyway.

The old abbey church, which became the Collegiate Church of Saint Gertrude under the canonesses, continues this modern tradition and has managed to become the headquarters of KittySaver. Today the basement of the old abbey holds a number of artifacts and the offices of our community.

How we help?

Every day, our team works to save the lives of kittens and cats by providing spay/neuter, trap/neuter/return (TNR), adoptions, wellness care, medical care, and community shelter support. Our staff and board are committed to working every day to protect and improve the lives of cats everywhere.
